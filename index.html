Introduction

  • Mechanical ventilation is a treatment in intensive care units for severe breathing problems
  • If the ventilator delivers breaths that are too big the patient may be harmed
  • Research has shown that tidal volumes (breath sizes) of about 6ml/kg ideal body weight minimise this risk
  • However, over a third of Americans are obese
  • Using actual body weight instead of ideal body weight to set ventilators risks substantial overventilation and lung damage
  • Ideal body weight can be estimated from a patient's height and gender using Robinson's equation

\[ Ideal\: body\: weight\: (men) = 52 kg\: + 1.9\: kg\: per\: inch\: over\: 5\: feet \]

Example

For example, an obese man might weight 110 kg. If he were 165cm tall his body mass index (BMI) would be:-

\[ BMI = \frac {weight(kg)}{height(m)^2} \]
